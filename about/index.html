About me.

I'm a (mostly) self-taught game dev and 3d artist from Frankfurt am Main, Germany.
I got my hands on blender about five(-ish) years ago to make custom scenery objects for NoLimits2. My first attempts were.. rough, but a few thousand hours later I'd consider myself pretty damn good.
On the way I learned texturing and picked up Substance Painter, which I've been using for about three-ish years [since ~mid 2020].

2020 - 2021 : CoasterGalaxyWorld.

Around early-mid 2020 I was approached by the owner of CoasterGalaxyWorld [abbrev. CGW] and joined their team. I started off at CGW with trackwork in NoLimits2. TL;DR - I designed virtual rollercoaster tracks that followed a vertex-controlled b-spline.

> More info about NoLimits2 can be found here.

Alongside trackwork I learned more about 3d modelling and took on the role of lead technical 3d artist around early 2021.
In addition to that I learned general level design practices and how to texture models in Substance Painter, also becoming the lead texture artist.

During my time at CGW I contributed to 7 of their projects and actively helped ship 3 (and a half) of them.

> More about that in "projects I've helped ship"

I left the team around mid-2021 with the intention to do freelance/hobby work because of repeated abuse and unrealistic time demands for no compensation.

2021 - 2023 : where I'm at now.

In August of 2021, I was approached by some members of CGW that had started their own side project. I can't say much about that project yet, but our team of six have all been working on it in our own time for fun and making slow but steady progress.
My role in development is mostly 3d modelling and texturing, though I occasionally write some simple code as well.

On my own I did the occasional model as a study/for practice, some of which can be seen overe here.

Since 2020 I've also been working on VRChat avatars and steadily improved my character modelling skills.

What are my strengths?

> hardsurface- and poly-modelling.
> complex organic shapes with good topology.
> sticking to a limit for poly-count (within reason).
> clean and space-efficient UVMaps
> simple rigging and skinning.
> good-looking and detailed PBR textures.
> adapting to a project's artstyle, no matter if it's toon or realism.
